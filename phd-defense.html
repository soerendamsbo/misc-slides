The project

  • Research project about how voters react to climate change and weather

  • Personal experiences are easier to understand. And it is an easier route to understanding climate change for everyone experiencing it. More and more people, more and more frequently, more and more intensely.

  • Voters increasingly experiences climate change directly through the weather. But they are still influenced by traditional sources as well, notably media coverage (politicians, activists, etc.). They may also point to weather events and make them visible as climate-related events.

    • Encounters new climate information all the time
    • Increasingly in extreme weather events
    • More and more often, they are attributed to climate change by scientists
    • But no matter what, there is a growing sense that we are feeling the consequences of climate change, regardless of attribution of individual events (subjective attritution or association)
    • This means that we experience climate change.
