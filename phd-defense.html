Results


  • OLS model suggests a 0.18-0.26 %-points effect of flooding
  • Flooding increases the election chances of pro-climate candidates
  • Translates into ~3 %-points higher election chances for the median candidate, if they are pro-climate

Mechanism

  • Voters with flooding experience prefer pro-climate candidates
  • Mostly due to issue salience and candidate communication
  • Not driven by supply-side:
    • converted climate candidates (Section 11.16)
    • party promotion of climate candidates
    • mobilization of new candidates
  • Nor by overlap with incumbent candidates
  • Nor by overlap with pro-welfare candidates

  • ~1-3 %-points higher pro-climate party vote share and candidate election chances
  • Climate-related issues become more salient
  • Voters use party issue ownership and candidate communication to translate heightened salience into a pro-climate vote
